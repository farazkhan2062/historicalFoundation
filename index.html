Pre‑Pakistan Education System (712–1947)

Education in the subcontinent began with the arrival of Muslims in 712 AD. Maktabs and Madrasahs taught Qur’an, Arabic, Persian, mathematics, astronomy, and medicine. The Mughal era marked a golden age of education, while British rule introduced modern English‑based education and universities.

Sir Syed Ahmed Khan – Educational Reformer

Sir Syed Ahmed Khan (1817–1898) pioneered modern Muslim education. He founded the Muhammadan Anglo‑Oriental College in 1875, later Aligarh Muslim University. His efforts awakened Muslim society intellectually and socially.

Education After Independence (1947–Present)

After independence, Pakistan faced challenges such as illiteracy and lack of institutions. Major milestones include the 1947 Education Conference, 1959 Education Commission, establishment of HEC, and Article 25‑A ensuring free education for children.

Education During Bhutto Era (1972–1977)

During Zulfikar Ali Bhutto’s government, education was nationalized and declared free. Enrollment increased, but challenges such as shortage of resources, political interference, weak administration, and decline in standards also emerged.

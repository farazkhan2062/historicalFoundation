Pre-Pakistan Education System (712–1947)

Education in the subcontinent began with the Muslim arrival in 712 AD. Maktabs and Madrasahs focused on Qur’an, Arabic, Persian, mathematics, and science. During the Mughal era, education flourished, while British rule introduced modern English-based education and universities.

Sir Syed Ahmed Khan & Educational Reforms

Sir Syed Ahmed Khan was a visionary reformer who promoted modern scientific and English education among Muslims. He founded the Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College in 1875, which later became Aligarh Muslim University. His educational movement awakened Muslim society intellectually and politically.

Education After Independence (1947–Present)

After independence, Pakistan faced serious challenges such as shortage of schools and trained teachers. Important milestones include the 1947 Education Conference, the 1959 Education Commission, nationalization in the 1970s, Islamization in the 1980s, and the 18th Amendment (2010) ensuring free education for children aged 5–16.

Education During Bhutto Era (1972–1977)

During Bhutto’s era, education was declared free and many private institutions were nationalized. Although enrollment increased, challenges such as shortage of resources, political interference, decline in standards, and weak administration negatively affected educational quality.

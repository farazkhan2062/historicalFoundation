Pre-Pakistan Education System (712–1947)

Education in the subcontinent began with the arrival of Muslims in 712 AD. Maktabs and Madrasahs focused on Qur’an, Arabic, Persian, mathematics, astronomy, and medicine. The Mughal period is considered the golden age of education, while British rule introduced English-based modern education and universities.

Sir Syed Ahmed Khan and Educational Reforms

Sir Syed Ahmed Khan (1817–1898) was the pioneer of modern Muslim education. He founded the Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College in 1875, which later became Aligarh Muslim University. His educational movement awakened Muslims intellectually and socially.

Education After Independence (1947–Present)

After independence, Pakistan faced serious challenges such as illiteracy and lack of institutions. Major reforms include the 1947 Education Conference, 1959 Education Commission, establishment of the Higher Education Commission (HEC), and Article 25-A of the Constitution.

Education During Bhutto Era (1972–1977)

During Zulfikar Ali Bhutto’s era, education was nationalized and declared free. Enrollment increased significantly, but challenges such as shortage of resources, political interference, weak administration, and decline in quality also emerged.
